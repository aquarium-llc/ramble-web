Child Safety Standards (CSAE)

Last updated: March 1, 2026

This page contains Ramble's externally published standards against child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE).

Zero-Tolerance Policy

Ramble has zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation. Any account that engages in CSAE, including the creation, distribution, or promotion of child sexual abuse material (CSAM), is subject to immediate enforcement action.

Scope

These standards apply to all user activity and user-generated surfaces in Ramble, including:

  • Profile photos, names, and usernames
  • Friend and social interactions
  • Behavior during live audio conversations
  • Any other activity facilitated through the app

Prohibited Content and Conduct

Ramble prohibits content or behavior that includes or promotes:

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM), including links or attempts to share it
  • Sexualization, grooming, or exploitation of minors
  • Sextortion, trafficking, or coercion involving minors
  • Solicitation of sexual content from minors
  • Any attempt to endanger children on or through the platform

In-App Reporting and Safety Tools

Ramble allows users to report child safety concerns in-app. Users can also email safety reports to support@ramble.co with relevant details.

Users can also use in-app safety controls such as blocking or removing other users.

Review and Enforcement

When we receive a child safety report, we prioritize review and may:

  • Remove violating profiles or content
  • Suspend or permanently terminate offending accounts
  • Restrict account access while an investigation is underway
  • Preserve relevant data as required by law

Reporting to Authorities and Legal Compliance

Ramble complies with applicable child safety laws and reporting obligations. Where required by law, we report apparent CSAM or child exploitation activity to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and/or relevant regional or national law enforcement authorities.

Age Requirements

Ramble is intended for users age 13 and older. We do not knowingly permit children under 13 to use the service.

Emergency Situations

If a child is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services immediately.

Contact

For child safety concerns, contact: support@ramble.co
